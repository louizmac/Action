## ⚡️ The Problem
Dragging files between windows, spaces, and full-screen apps on macOS can be frustrating. You often run out of "hands" or have to hover over Dock icons while holding a heavy file.

## ✨ The Solution
**Action** provides a temporary "shelf" that lives in the corner of your screen. Whenever you start dragging a file, a beautiful glassmorphic window appears. Drop it there, switch your workspace, and pull it out when you're ready.

## 🛠 Installation

1. **Download** the latest `.app` from the Releases page.
2. **Move** `Action.app` to your `/Applications` folder.
3. **Launch** the app. It will appear in your Menu Bar.
4. **Grant Permissions:** If prompted, ensure the app has the necessary permissions to monitor drag events.

> **Tip:** Add Action to your **Login Items** in System Settings so it's always ready when you start your Mac.

## 🕹 Usage

1. **Pick up** any file or group of files in Finder (or any other app).
2. **Move** your cursor. The Action shelf will automatically slide in from the bottom-left.
3. **Drop** the files into the shelf.
4. **Navigate** to your destination (change Space, open a new app).
5. **Drag out** the files from the shelf to their final location.
6. Click the <kbd>X</kbd> to clear a specific item or empty the shelf.
